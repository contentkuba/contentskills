## The Strategy Problem

Most content strategies fail because they're:
- Platform-first ("We need a TikTok strategy") rather than goal-first
- Too broad ("Create valuable content for our audience")
- Copy-paste from a competitor without understanding why it works for them
- Ignored after week two because they're not realistic

A real content strategy answers:
1. What business outcome does content support?
2. Who are we trying to reach, and where are they already?
3. What do we have to say that's different?
4. What can we actually sustain?

## Strategy Interview

Ask in conversation:

### Business Context
- What's the primary business goal content should support? (brand awareness / lead gen / sales / retention / community)
- What's the timeline? (next 90 days / 6 months / ongoing)
- What does success look like in concrete terms? (follower growth / email signups / inbound leads / revenue)

### Current State
- What content are you currently creating? What's working?
- What platforms are active?
- What resources exist? (Solo creator / small team / budget for tools or freelancers)
- How many hours per week can realistically go to content?

### Competitive Landscape
- Who in your space is doing content well? What specifically works for them?
- What are they NOT doing? Where's the gap?

### Differentiation
- What's the one POV or angle that only you can credibly hold?
- What does your audience not hear enough of in your space?

## Strategy Output

### 1. Content Mission Statement
One sentence that defines: who the content is for, what it does for them, and how it's different.

Format: "We create [format] for [audience] who [situation], so they can [outcome] — without [common alternative that doesn't work]."

### 2. Content Pillars (3–5)
The recurring themes everything maps back to. Each pillar should:
- Connect to the audience's real problems
- Reflect the brand's genuine expertise
- Be sustainable to create content about consistently

For each pillar:
- Name
- What it addresses for the audience
- What it signals about the brand
- Example content types that fit

### 3. Platform Strategy
For each active platform:
- Primary purpose (awareness / depth / community / conversion)
- Content type that fits
- Posting frequency
- How it connects to other platforms

### 4. Content Mix
The ratio of content types. Suggested framework:
- 60% Educational / value (builds trust)
- 25% Story / personality (builds connection)
- 15% Promotional / conversion (drives action)

Adjust for the brand's goal and voice.

### 5. Repurposing Map
How one piece of content becomes many. Define the primary format and the downstream chain.

Example:
Long-form blog → Newsletter summary → LinkedIn post → Carousel → Reel hook → X thread

### 6. 90-Day Execution Plan
Realistic posting schedule with:
- Platform commitments
- Content type per slot
- Batching recommendations
- Review/iteration points

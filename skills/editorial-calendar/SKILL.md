## Calendar Fundamentals

A calendar is an execution tool. It fails when it's:
- Over-ambitious (daily posting for a solo creator with a full-time job)
- Platform-agnostic (no specificity on what kind of content goes where)
- Topic-only (no format, no angle, no hook direction)
- Rigid (no room for reactive or trend content)

A working calendar has:
- Defined platforms and frequencies
- Content types per slot (not just "LinkedIn post" but "LinkedIn story post" or "LinkedIn how-to")
- Pillar balance (content doesn't cluster around one theme)
- Buffer slots for reactive content
- Repurposing chains visible (where does each piece get reused?)

## Calendar Interview

Ask only what's not already in strategy/brand-voice:
- What timeframe? (1 week / 2 weeks / 1 month / 90 days)
- What platforms are in scope?
- What's the posting frequency per platform?
- Are there any fixed dates? (launches, events, seasonal moments)
- What content already exists and can be repurposed?

## Calendar Structure Options

**Weekly Template**
Repeating weekly structure. Best for creators with stable routines.
Define: what type of content goes in each slot, every week.

**Monthly Calendar**
Day-by-day plan for 30 days. Includes specific topics and formats.
Best for: launch periods, campaigns, or onboarding a new content rhythm.

**90-Day Roadmap**
Thematic blocks by month with weekly cadence defined.
Best for: strategic planning, agency clients, quarterly goal alignment.

## Calendar Output Format

### Weekly Template

```
WEEKLY CONTENT TEMPLATE: [Brand Name]

Monday: [Platform] — [Content type] — [Pillar]
Wednesday: [Platform] — [Content type] — [Pillar]
Friday: [Platform] — [Content type] — [Pillar]
[Newsletter: every X weeks]

Weekly time budget:
- Writing: [X hours]
- Recording: [X hours]
- Editing/publishing: [X hours]
Total: [X hours/week]
```

## Batching Recommendations

Suggest a batching schedule alongside the calendar:
- "Write all LinkedIn posts in one 2-hour session on [day]"
- "Record all Reels in one filming session every 2 weeks"
- "Newsletter drafted every [day], published [day]"

Batching is how solo creators maintain consistency without burning out.

## Seasonal & Campaign Overlays

If the brand has:
- Product launches → flag the pre/during/post content windows
- Industry events → content that rides awareness
- Seasonal moments → relevant to the audience (not just generic holidays)

Mark these as **campaign weeks** with different content structure than regular weeks.

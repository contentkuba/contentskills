## Context Check

Read `.agents/brand-voice.md`. Distribution channels and tactics must match the brand's audience location and voice. Spam-like distribution is always off-brand.

## The Distribution Problem

Most creators spend 90% of their time creating and 10% distributing. The ratio should be closer to 50/50. A great piece with no distribution strategy is a tree falling in an empty forest.

## Distribution Channels

### Owned Channels
Channels the brand controls:
- Email list / newsletter (highest conversion, most reliable reach)
- Website / blog
- Existing social following
- Community / Discord / Slack

### Earned Channels
Reach through relationships and quality:
- Shares from others (organic amplification)
- Press / media mentions
- Guest posts or contributor slots
- Podcast appearances
- Community reposts

### Distribution Networks
Places where content can be submitted or shared:
- Relevant Slack communities and Discord servers
- Reddit (relevant subreddits — must follow community norms)
- LinkedIn groups
- Facebook groups
- Niche newsletters (as a link or featured piece)
- Content aggregators (Hacker News, Product Hunt, Indie Hackers, etc. — topic dependent)
- Quora / Stack Exchange (answer questions, link to content where genuinely helpful)

### Paid Amplification (if budget exists)
- LinkedIn post boost
- Newsletter sponsor slot (in relevant newsletters)
- Content distribution tools (Quuu, etc.)

## Distribution Plan by Content Type

**LinkedIn Post**
1. Post natively (no link — LinkedIn suppresses link posts)
2. First comment: add any links
3. Engage with every reply in first hour (boosts algorithm)
4. DM 3–5 specific people who would find it relevant (not a blast)
5. Cross-post to Threads or X (adapted)
6. Add to newsletter as "from the feed this week"

**Blog Post / Article**
1. Publish on primary blog
2. Newsletter: feature excerpt + link
3. LinkedIn: post the best insight from the article, link in comments
4. X: thread version or single key quote
5. Submit to relevant communities (Slack, Reddit, Indie Hackers) — check rules first
6. Email 3–5 people who would genuinely benefit from reading it (personalized note)
7. Syndicate to Medium, Substack Notes, or Hashnode if relevant
8. Update old related content with internal links to the new piece

**YouTube Video**
1. Publish to YouTube with optimized title, description, tags, chapters
2. Create a Reel/Short from the best 30–60 seconds
3. LinkedIn post: the core insight from the video (with link in comments)
4. Newsletter: video summary + link
5. Pin comment on the video with resources mentioned
6. Share in relevant communities with context (not just "check out my video")

**Newsletter Issue**
1. Send to list
2. Post best section to LinkedIn / X / Threads as standalone content
3. Archive on website (SEO value + shareable link)
4. Share in Substack Notes or Beehiiv's recommendation feature

## Distribution Timing

**Social posts:** Engagement within first 60 minutes matters most. Be available to reply immediately after posting.

**Newsletter:** Tuesday–Thursday, morning sends typically outperform. Test for the specific list.

**Community sharing:** Check community peak hours. Share when the audience is online, not at the convenience of the scheduler.

**Evergreen republishing:** Reshare high-performing content every 3–6 months. Social media audiences are 90% different than they were 6 months ago.

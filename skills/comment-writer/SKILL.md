## Why Strategic Commenting Works

Commenting on the right posts, in the right way, puts your name in front of:
- The post author (relationship building)
- Their entire audience (many of whom haven't seen your content)
- The algorithm (engagement signals amplify both posts)

On LinkedIn especially, a great comment on a high-performing post can drive more profile visits than an average original post.

## The Comment Quality Test

A valuable comment does at least one of:
- Adds a perspective or angle the post didn't cover
- Shares a relevant personal experience or data point
- Respectfully challenges or nuances a claim
- Asks a genuinely interesting follow-up question
- Validates the post's insight with a specific, non-generic example

A low-value comment does:
- Generic agreement ("Great post! So true!")
- Pure compliment ("Love this!")
- Self-promotion ("I wrote about this too, check my profile")
- One-word reactions ("Exactly!")
- Repeating what the post already said

## Comment Types

### The Additive Comment
Add something the post missed.
Structure: [Brief acknowledgment of the post's point] + [New angle or additional insight] + [Optional: question to extend the discussion]

### The Experiential Comment
Share a real example or story.
Structure: [What the post made me think of] + [Specific personal example or story, 2–4 sentences] + [What I took from it]

### The Respectful Challenge
Gently push back or add nuance.
Structure: [Acknowledge the valid point] + [Where you see it differently or a context where it's more complex] + [Keep it curious, not combative]

### The Expert Deepdive
Add technical depth on a specific point.
Structure: [The specific point you're building on] + [Deeper explanation, example, or data] + [Keep it accessible — you're adding, not showing off]

### The Question Comment
Ask something that extends the discussion and shows engagement.
Structure: [Brief context on why you're asking] + [One specific, interesting question]
Not: "What do you think?" (too vague)
Yes: "You mentioned X — how did you approach Y when [specific scenario]?"

## Platform Differences

**LinkedIn**
- 2–5 sentences is the sweet spot
- Line breaks between thoughts if over 3 sentences
- Avoid hashtags in comments
- Engaging with smaller creators is often higher ROI than big names (your comment is more visible)

**X (Twitter)**
- 1–3 sentences
- Can be more direct / punchy
- Quote tweets allow slightly longer treatment
- Wit is valued

**Instagram**
- Shorter — 1–3 sentences
- Emojis acceptable depending on brand voice
- Most comments are surface-level; any genuine engagement stands out

## Target Selection

Help the user identify where to comment for maximum brand-building:
- Posts from ideal audience members (future clients, followers)
- Posts from peers in adjacent spaces (cross-pollination)
- Posts from thought leaders the target audience follows
- High-performing posts in the niche (while they're trending, not days later)

Avoid:
- Competitors (unless the brand deliberately engages in that way)
- Controversial political content
- Posts where the comment section is already hostile

## Comment Output Format

When given a post to comment on, deliver:

```
POST: [Summary of what the post is about]
Comment type: [Additive / Experiential / Challenge / Expert / Question]

COMMENT:
[The comment text, platform-formatted]

ALTERNATIVE (if first feels weak):
[Second option]

NOTE: [Post while the post is fresh — ideally within first 2 hours of posting]
```

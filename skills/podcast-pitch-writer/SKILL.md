## Why Most Pitches Fail

The average podcast pitch:
- Is about the guest, not the audience
- Lists credentials without explaining relevance
- Proposes generic topics that 50 other guests could cover
- Shows no evidence of having listened to the show

A great pitch:
- Proves the guest knows the show
- Makes the host see a specific, compelling episode
- Shows the audience gets value, not just the guest

## Before Writing the Pitch

Collect from the user:
- Which podcast are they pitching?
- Have they listened to it? Which episodes specifically?
- What is their unique angle or topic?
- What specific insight or framework would they share?
- What do they want the audience to walk away with?
- What credentials are most relevant to this specific show?
- Who are the typical guests? (positioning context)

## Pitch Structure

### Subject Line
Not: "Guest Pitch: [Your Name]"
Yes: "[Episode hook] — Guest pitch from [Name]"

Example: "Why most content strategies fail at distribution — guest pitch from [Name]"

The subject line is the first hook. Make it about the idea.

### Opening: Show You Listen
Reference a specific episode, guest, or recurring theme on the show. Be specific — name the episode, name the moment, say why it resonated.

1–2 sentences. Not a compliment parade. Specific and genuine.

### The Pitch: Lead with the Episode Idea
Before credentials, give the host a clear picture of the episode:
- The specific topic or question the episode would explore
- The counterintuitive angle or surprising insight
- Why *now* (is this timely? connected to a trend?)

This section answers: "What episode am I getting?"

### The Angle (Why This, Why Now)
Why is this topic important for their specific audience? What does the listener walk away with?

### The Guest (Why You)
Now, and only now, establish credentials:
- Relevant experience (specific, not generic)
- Unique vantage point (why can only *you* deliver this angle?)
- Social proof (audience size, publications, notable work — only what's relevant)

Keep this short. The idea sells first; credentials support it.

### The Ask
Clear and simple:
- Available to record [dates or general availability]
- Happy to [fill out a form / send more / jump on a pre-call]
- One specific ask, not a paragraph of options

## Pitch Output

```
PODCAST PITCH: [Show Name]
Host: [Name]
Episode concept: [One line]

---

SUBJECT LINE: [Option 1]
SUBJECT LINE ALT: [Option 2]

---

PITCH BODY:

Hi [Host name],

[Show reference — specific episode or theme, 1–2 sentences]

[Episode idea paragraph — what the episode is, what the angle is, why now]

[Audience value — what listeners walk away with]

[Guest credentials — 3–4 sentences max, only what's relevant]

Happy to send over a full bio, topic outline, or jump on a quick call to see if it's a fit. I'm flexible on scheduling — whenever works for you.

[Name]
[Link to website or social proof]

---

FOLLOW-UP EMAIL (for non-response after 7–10 days):
[Brief, no-guilt, one additional angle or value add]
```

## Pitch Personalization Checklist

Before sending, verify:
- [ ] Subject line is about the idea, not the guest
- [ ] Show reference is specific (episode name/number, not "I love your show")
- [ ] Episode idea is clear in 2–3 sentences
- [ ] Credentials are relevant to THIS show's audience
- [ ] The host can picture the episode
- [ ] One clear ask at the end

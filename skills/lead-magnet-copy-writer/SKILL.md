## Lead Magnet Concept Check

Before writing copy, validate the concept:

A strong lead magnet is:
- **Specific:** "The LinkedIn Hook Formula" not "LinkedIn Tips"
- **Fast to consume:** Checklists, templates, and short guides outperform long ebooks
- **Immediately applicable:** Subscriber can use it today
- **Directly relevant to what you sell:** The freebie attracts the same person as the offer

Ask the user:
- What is the lead magnet? (format: checklist / template / guide / mini-course / tool / swipe file)
- What specific problem does it solve?
- Who specifically is it for?
- What does someone walk away able to do that they couldn't before?
- What do you sell, and how does this connect?

If the concept is weak (too vague, too broad, no clear audience), flag it and suggest refinements before writing copy.

## Landing Page Copy

### Headline
The most important line. Answer: "What do I get, and why do I want it?"

Formulas:
- "[Specific result] in [time / steps]"
- "The [adjective] [format] for [audience] who want to [outcome]"
- "How to [outcome] without [common sacrifice]"
- "Get [specific thing] — free"

Generate 5–7 options, recommend top 2.

### Subheadline
1–2 sentences that add specificity or urgency. What specifically is inside? Why now?

### Bullet Points (The Promise)
3–5 bullets, each finishing: "So you can..."
Not feature bullets ("Includes a 10-page guide")
Benefit bullets ("The exact framework I used to grow from 0 to 5,000 subscribers in 6 months — so you can stop guessing and start compounding")

### Social Proof (if available)
One testimonial or data point before the opt-in form.

### The Form
Above the fold or close to it. The CTA button text matters:
- Not: "Submit" / "Subscribe"
- Yes: "Send me the [name of thing]" / "Get instant access" / "Yes, I want [specific outcome]"

### Below the Form (optional)
- What happens next (set expectations)
- Privacy statement (brief: "No spam. Unsubscribe anytime.")

## Opt-In Widget Copy (Short Version)

For embedding in blog posts, sidebar, pop-ups:
- Headline: 5–8 words
- 1–2 sentences on what they get
- CTA button text

## Delivery Email

The first email they receive. Most creators send a terrible "here's your thing" email. This is a wasted moment.

Structure:
- Subject line: "[Name of thing] is on its way — plus one thing to do first"
- Open: A brief, personal note (not "Congratulations on subscribing!")
- Deliver: Clear link to the resource
- Set expectations: "Every [frequency], I send [what the newsletter is]"
- One soft next step: Read this, try this, reply with X
- Warm close — in the brand's voice

## Social Promotion Copy

1 LinkedIn post + 1 X post + 1 Instagram caption driving to the lead magnet. Each should:
- Lead with the problem the lead magnet solves (not the freebie)
- Be specific about what's inside
- Include a clear CTA with the link / bio link
